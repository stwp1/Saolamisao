WHITE PROTESTANTS AND CATHOLICS SUPPORT TRUMP, BUT VOTERS IN OTHER U.S. RELIGIOUS GROUPS PREFER HARRIS

Support for Donald Trump among White Protestants and Catholics remains strong, reflecting a pattern seen
in previous elections where these groups have favored conservative leadership.

Trump's policies and values resonate with many within these religious communities,
particularly on issues like abortion, religious freedom, and traditional family values.

A new survey reveals a stark religious divide in the 2024 presidential race,
with white Christians favoring Trump and other groups backing Harris,
as economy and immigration emerge as critical issues.

Heading into the fall campaign for president, U.S. religious groups
that traditionally have leaned Republican are backing former President Donald Trump by wide margins,
while religious groups that traditionally have favored Democratic candidates
are mostly supporting Vice President Kamala Harris.

RELIGIOUS LEADERS IN PHILIPPINES REAFFIRM COMMITMENT TO PEACE AND JUSTICE

Religious leaders in Mindanao have reaffirmed their commitment to peace and sustainable development
during the Mindanao Religious Leaders Conference (MiRLeC), held last week in Davao City.

The gathering, which brought together key figures from different religious communities,
culminated in a declaration emphasizing the role of faith in fostering peace in the conflict-affected region.

“We are peacemakers. Peace based on justice is our duty,”
read the final declaration of the conference.
